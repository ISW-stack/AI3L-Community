# Member User Guide

> **Role:** Member — a registered user with full access to all community features.
>
> Members can create content, participate in discussions, send direct messages, manage social connections, and collaborate with others. This guide covers everything a Member can do **beyond** what is available to Guests. Read the [Guest Guide](guest-guide.md) first for browsing basics.

## Home Dashboard (Member View)

The home dashboard expands for Members:

- **Welcome card** with quick links to "Browse Forum" and "My SIGs".
- **Trending posts** — top 3 most-discussed posts with direct links.
- **Recent posts** — the 5 newest posts.
- **Right sidebar:**
  - Unread notifications banner (if any).
  - Friend Recommendations widget — suggested users to connect with; click to view profile or dismiss.
  - Community Stats (members, posts, SIGs).
  - Your SIGs — up to 5 SIGs you belong to, with member counts.
  - Featured SIGs — highlighted groups to explore.
  - Quick Links: Create Post, Browse SIGs, Edit Profile.

## Forum — Creating and Managing Posts

### Creating a Post

1. Click the **floating "+" button** on the Forum page, or use the Quick Links on the home dashboard.
2. Fill in the post form:
   - **Title** (required)
   - **Category** — select from the dropdown.
   - **SIG** — optionally publish under a SIG you belong to.
   - **Content** — rich-text editor (TipTap) with formatting tools:
     - Bold, italic, headings (H1–H3), bullet/numbered lists
     - Links, blockquotes, code blocks, horizontal rules
     - Tables (insert, add/remove rows and columns)
   - **Keywords** — up to 15 tags, each max 50 characters.
   - **Allow Comments** — checkbox (enabled by default).
3. Click **Publish**.

**Auto-save drafts:** Your post is automatically saved as a draft every second as you type. Drafts are per-SIG and per-user. If you leave and return, a banner offers to restore or discard the draft.

**Navigation guard:** If you try to leave with unsaved changes, a confirmation dialog appears.

### Editing a Post

1. Open your post and click **Edit**.
2. The content switches to an inline editor. Modify the title and body.
3. Click **Save**. Each edit creates a new version.

**Version conflict:** If someone else edits the post while you are editing, the system warns you.

### Viewing Edit History

- On your own post (or any post if you are Admin+), click **View Edit History**.
- A modal shows all versions with content differences.

### Deleting a Post

- On your own post, click **Delete** and confirm in the modal.
- Deleted posts are soft-deleted and no longer visible.

### Co-Authors

You can invite other members to be credited as co-authors on your posts.

1. Open your post and find the **Co-Author Manager** section.
2. Search for a user by name and send an invitation.
3. You can also add **external co-authors** (people not on the platform) with a name and affiliation.
4. Invited users see the invitation in **Profile > Social tab** and can Accept or Decline.
5. Accepted co-authors appear on the post with their avatars and affiliations.
6. Co-authors can leave a co-authorship; the post owner can remove co-authors.

### Citations

Posts can reference other posts, creating a citation network.

- In the post editor, use the **citation search** to find and link to other posts.
- On any post detail page, see "Cited by N posts" and "References N posts" sections.
- Self-citations are marked with a badge.

### Reporting a Post

If you find content that violates community guidelines:

1. Open the post and click the **Report** button.
2. Enter a reason in the textarea.
3. Submit. An admin will review the report.

## Q&A — Questions and Answers

### Asking a Question

1. Navigate to **Q&A** and click the **floating "+" button** or "Ask a Question".
2. Fill in:
   - **Title** (required)
   - **Content** — describe your question using the rich-text editor.
   - **Category** — select a relevant category.
   - **Keywords** — up to 15 tags.
3. Publish. Auto-save drafts work the same way as forum posts.

### Answering a Question

1. Open a question and scroll to the bottom.
2. Write your answer in the rich-text editor.
3. Submit.

### Voting

- Each answer has **upvote** and **downvote** buttons.
- Answers are sorted by vote score (highest first), with the best answer pinned to the top.
- You can change your vote by clicking the opposite arrow.

### Marking the Best Answer

If you are the **question author**:

1. Find the answer that best resolves your question.
2. Click the **checkmark** button to mark it as the Best Answer.
3. The answer moves to the top with a "Best Answer" badge.
4. You can unmark it later if a better answer appears.

## Comments and Reactions

### Commenting on Posts

1. Open a post and scroll to the **Comments** section.
2. Type your comment in the input area and submit.
3. To **reply** to an existing comment, click the Reply button on that comment. Your reply will be nested under it.

### Editing and Deleting Comments

- **Edit:** Click Edit on your own comment, modify the text, and save.
- **Delete:** Click Delete on your own comment and confirm.

### Emoji Reactions

- Posts and comments have a **reaction picker**.
- Click the reaction area to open the emoji selector and toggle reactions.
- Each reaction shows its count and who reacted (on hover).

## Special Interest Groups (SIGs)

### Joining a SIG

1. Browse the **SIG Directory** (`/sigs`).
2. Click a SIG to view its detail page.
3. Click **Join** on the SIG header card.
4. You are now a SIG Member and can post within this SIG.

### Leaving a SIG

- On the SIG detail page, click **Leave**.
- If you are the sole SIG Admin, you must transfer or assign another admin before leaving.

### Posting in a SIG

- On the SIG's Posts tab, click the **floating "+" button**.
- The post creation form opens with this SIG pre-selected.

### Quick Comment Panel

- On the SIG Posts tab, each post card has an expand button to preview comments and write a quick reply without navigating to the full post detail.

### Copying a SIG Share Link

- Click **Copy Share Link** on the SIG header to copy the URL to your clipboard.

### SIG Roles

Within each SIG, members have one of three roles:

| SIG Role | Permissions |
|---|---|
| **Member** | View content, post, comment within the SIG. |
| **Sub-Admin** | Member permissions + create SIG forms, manage members (remove, promote/demote below own level). |
| **Admin** | Full SIG control: all Sub-Admin permissions + edit SIG settings, assign/demote Sub-Admins. |

SIG Admins are typically the SIG creator or users promoted by a platform Admin/Super Admin.

Platform Admins and Super Admins automatically have SIG Admin-level access to all SIGs.

### SIG Forms

SIG Admins and Sub-Admins can create forms scoped to their SIG. See [Forms — Creating and Managing](#forms--creating-and-managing) for details.

## Forms — Creating and Managing

### Creating a Form

Members can create **standalone forms** (not tied to a SIG):

1. Navigate to **Forms** and click **Create Form**.
2. Configure form metadata:
   - **Title** (required)
   - **Description** — rich-text editor.
   - **Banner image** — optional header image.
   - **Deadline** — date and time after which submissions close.
   - **Max respondents** — optional cap on total responses (enforced atomically).
   - **Lock schema** — prevent question edits after the first response.
   - **Allow non-members** — if the form is within a SIG, whether non-SIG-members can submit.

### Building Questions

The form builder supports drag-and-drop question management:

- **Add** questions using the "Add Question" button.
- **Reorder** by dragging questions (touch-supported).
- **Duplicate** a question to create a copy.
- **Delete** a question.
- **Collapse/Expand** all questions for overview.

#### Question Types

| Type | Description | Options |
|---|---|---|
| **Text** | Single-line text input | Placeholder, max length |
| **Textarea** | Multi-line text input | Placeholder, max length |
| **Single Choice** | Radio buttons — one selection | Add/remove/reorder options |
| **Multiple Choice** | Checkboxes — multiple selections | Add/remove/reorder options |
| **Dropdown** | Select menu — one selection | Add/remove/reorder options |
| **Rating** | Numeric scale (e.g., 1–5) | Min value, max value, min label, max label |
| **File Upload** | File attachment | Allowed file types, max file size |

Each question can be marked as **Required** (respondents must answer before submitting).

### Undo/Redo

The form builder maintains an undo/redo history. Use the toolbar buttons to step backward or forward through changes.

### Live Preview

- A side panel shows a real-time preview of your form as respondents will see it.
- Toggle between desktop and mobile viewport modes.

### Draft Auto-Save

Form builder state is auto-saved. If you close and reopen, a "Draft restored" banner appears with the option to discard.

### Editing a Form

1. Open the form and click **Edit** (visible to the form creator or platform/SIG admins).
2. Modify questions or metadata.
3. Save. If "Lock schema" is enabled and responses exist, question structure cannot be changed.

### Viewing Responses

1. On a form you created (or administer), click **View Responses**.
2. Two tabs are available:
   - **Individual** — browse each respondent's answers, filterable by search text and date range.
   - **Statistics** — aggregated data:
     - Choice questions: bar charts with percentages.
     - Rating questions: average score with distribution bars.
     - Text questions: expandable list of responses.
     - File upload: count of uploaded files.

### Exporting to CSV

1. Click **Export CSV** on a form you own or administer.
2. A background task (Celery) processes the export.
3. A progress modal appears; you can cancel if needed.
4. Once complete, the CSV file downloads automatically.

### Sharing a Form

- Click **Copy Share Link** to copy the form URL to your clipboard for distribution.

## Albums — Participation

### Joining an Album

1. Browse the **Albums Directory** (`/albums`).
2. Click an album to view its detail page.
3. Click **Join**. Depending on the album settings, you may need approval from the album admin.

### Uploading Photos

1. Open an album you are a member of.
2. On the Photos tab, click **Upload**.
3. In the upload modal, drag and drop files or click to select from your device.
4. Supported: image files and general files (within the 50 MB limit per file).
5. Uploaded files count toward your 1 GB storage quota.

### Photo Lightbox

- Click any photo to open the full-screen lightbox.
- Navigate between photos with previous/next arrows or keyboard.

### Album Comments

- On the Comments tab, write and read album-level comments.
- Comments support the same edit/delete actions as forum post comments.

## Direct Messages (DM)

Navigate to **Messages** via the envelope icon in the navbar, or click **Send Message** on a user's profile.

### Conversation List

The left panel shows all your conversations:

- Each entry displays the other user's avatar, name, last message preview, timestamp, and unread count badge.
- Click a conversation to open the message thread.
- On mobile, the list and thread are shown one at a time; use the back button to return to the list.

### Sending a Message

1. Open a conversation or start a new one from a user's profile.
2. Type your message in the text area at the bottom.
3. Press **Enter** or click the send button.

### File Attachments

1. Click the **attachment button** next to the text area.
2. Select a file (max 50 MB). The file counts toward your 1 GB storage quota.
3. Attachments are shown in the message bubble with filename, size, and a download link.
4. **Important:** File attachments expire after **3 days** and are automatically deleted.

### Editing a Message

- Click the **"..."** menu on your own message and select **Edit**.
- Modify the text and confirm.
- Editing is only available within **12 hours** of sending.

### Recalling a Message

- Click **"..."** > **Recall** on your own message.
- Confirm in the modal. The message is replaced with "Message recalled" for both parties.
- Recall is only available within **12 hours** of sending.

### Read Receipts

- A single checkmark (check) means the message was sent.
- Double checkmarks (check check) mean the message has been read by the recipient.

### Privacy Settings

- Toggle **"Open to all" / "Friends only"** at the top of the Messages page.
- When set to "Friends only," only users on your friends list can send you messages.
- A lock icon appears on your profile when this mode is active.

### Message Retention

| Content Type | Retention Period |
|---|---|
| Text messages | **7 days** (automatically deleted by a scheduled cleanup task) |
| File attachments | **3 days** (automatically deleted by a scheduled cleanup task) |

### Conversation Limits

Each conversation has a **50,000 character cap**. When the cap is reached, the oldest messages are automatically deleted to make room for new ones.

### Restrictions

- You cannot message yourself.
- You cannot message a user who has **blocked** you.
- If the recipient has "Friends only" enabled, you must be friends first.

## Social Features

### Friends

Navigate to **Friends** from the user menu or profile.

**Sending a Friend Request:**
1. Visit a user's profile.
2. Click the **Add Friend** button in the social actions area.
3. The user receives a notification and can accept or decline.

**Managing Friend Requests:**
- Go to **Friends** > **Friend Requests** tab.
- **Incoming** requests: Accept or Decline.
- **Outgoing** requests: visible with their status.

**Unfriending:**
- On the **Friends** tab, click **Unfriend** next to any friend.
- Confirm in the modal.

### Following

Navigate to **Following** from the user menu.

- **Following tab:** Users you follow. Click **Unfollow** to stop following.
- **Followers tab:** Users who follow you. Click **Follow Back** if you are not already following them.

To follow a user, visit their profile and click the **Follow** button.

### Blocking

Navigate to **Blocked Users** from the user menu.

**Blocking a user:**
1. Visit their profile.
2. Click **Block** in the social actions area.
3. Blocked users cannot see your profile, send you messages, or interact with you.

**Unblocking:**
- On the Blocked Users page, click **Unblock** next to the user and confirm.

### Social Actions on User Profiles

When viewing another user's profile, you see contextual buttons:

| Button | Action |
|---|---|
| Send Message | Opens a DM conversation with this user |
| Follow / Unfollow | Toggle following this user |
| Add Friend / Cancel Request | Send or cancel a friend request |
| Accept (if they sent you a request) | Accept their pending friend request |
| Block / Unblock | Toggle blocking this user |

A **DM lock icon** appears if the user has "Friends only" messaging enabled.

## About Page

Members have access to the About section (navbar dropdown: Introduction, Org Chart, Members).

### Introduction (`/about`)

- Platform overview with a banner photo and descriptive text (set by Super Admin).
- **Contributors grid** — cards showing contributor avatars, display names, and roles. Avatars are proxied through the backend (GitHub usernames are not exposed in the frontend).

### Org Chart (`/about/org-chart`)

- A hierarchical view organized by SIGs.
- Each SIG section shows:
  - SIG name and description.
  - Member list with role badges (Admin, Sub-Admin, Member).
  - Expandable to show all members (preview shows first 10).
- **SIG Admins/Sub-Admins** can edit their SIG's description and their own bio within the org chart.
- Regular members can edit their own bio if they belong to the SIG.

### Members Directory (`/about/members`)

- A searchable, paginated grid of all platform members (24 per page).
- Each card shows avatar, display name, role badge, and a bio snippet.
- Search with 300ms debounce by name.
- Click a card to navigate to that user's profile.

## Profile Management

Click your avatar in the navbar and select **Profile** to access the 4-tab profile page.

### General Tab

- **Avatar:** Upload a new profile picture (image file). A preview with your initials is shown if no avatar is set.
- **Display Name:** Your public name.
- **Bio:** Rich-text editor for a personal description.
- **Affiliation:** Your organization or institution.
- **ORCID:** Your academic identifier.
- **Username:** Read-only display.
- **Storage Usage:** A progress bar showing used space vs. your 1 GB quota.
- **Role Badge:** Your current platform role.
- Click **Save** to apply changes.

### Social Tab

- Quick links to **Friends**, **Following**, and **Blocked Users** pages.
- **Co-Author Invitations:** A list of pending invitations from other users to co-author their posts. Accept or Decline each invitation.

### Security Tab

- **Change Password:**
  1. Enter your current password.
  2. Enter a new password (must meet strength requirements).
  3. Confirm the new password.
  4. Submit. You will be automatically logged out after 1.5 seconds to re-authenticate.

- **Generate Invite Code:**
  1. Click "Generate Invite Code."
  2. A new code appears with a **Copy to Clipboard** button.
  3. Share this code with someone you want to invite to the platform.

### Danger Zone Tab

- **Delete Account:**
  1. Click "Delete Account."
  2. Confirm in the modal.
  3. Your account is permanently deleted and you are logged out.
  4. **Note:** You cannot delete your account if you are the sole Admin of any SIG. Transfer the admin role first.

### Viewing Other Users' Profiles

Navigate to `/users/:id` or click any user's name/avatar across the platform.

- See their avatar, display name, role, username, join date.
- Bio, affiliation, ORCID (if provided).
- Profile view counts (unique and total).
- **Posts tab:** Their authored posts.
- **Co-Authored Posts tab:** Posts they co-authored.
- Social action buttons (follow, friend, block, message).

## File Uploads and Storage

### Storage Quota

Every member has a **1 GB** total storage quota. This includes:
- Post editor file uploads (images, attachments)
- Album photo uploads
- DM file attachments
- Form file-upload responses

Check your usage on the **Profile > General tab** under the storage usage bar.

### Upload Limits

- Maximum file size per upload: **50 MB**.
- Album uploads support chunked reading for large files.

### Virus Scanning

- Uploaded files may be queued for VirusTotal scanning.
- While a scan is pending, the file returns a **202 status** and cannot be downloaded.
- Once cleared, the file becomes available normally.
- If a file is flagged, it may be quarantined by an admin.

### DM File Retention

DM file attachments are automatically deleted after **3 days** by a scheduled cleanup task. Download important files promptly.
